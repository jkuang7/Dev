# Aspida Interview Study Plan

Last updated: 2026-02-11

This is your single source of truth for prep.

## What to expect (likely rounds)

1. Recruiter screen (motivation + fit)
2. Hiring manager (ownership + execution judgment)
3. Technical/coding (problem solving + communication)
4. System design (architecture + tradeoffs + reliability)
5. Behavioral/panel (collaboration, leadership, pressure handling)

Typical interview length assumption: 30-45 minutes.
Prep for concise, high-signal answers first.

## Primary goal

- Be interview-ready with strong ownership narratives, startup-fit clarity, and practical execution judgment.
- Emphasize your preference for high-trust, low-bureaucracy environments with strong quality standards.
- De-risk the hiring decision by proving: reliable execution, technical depth, clear communication, and long-term culture fit.

## Weighted focus (optimized to your current gaps)

- 45% Behavioral and ownership narrative
- 40% System/integration design (your current weakest area)
- 15% Coding fluency refresh (maintain speed and comfort)

## Order of operations (updated)

- Phase 1 first: behavioral + resume narrative mastery
- Phase 2 second: one rough rep across full interview flow
- Phase 3 third: system design ramp and repetition
- Phase 4 ongoing: coding maintenance (Blind 50)

Why this order:
- Your first round is behavioral-heavy.
- Strong narrative increases conversion to later rounds.
- System design reps are most useful after story confidence is locked.

## Just-in-time prep protocol (round-based)

Use this whenever a new round gets scheduled.

- If next round is recruiter/HM:
  - 70% behavioral + narrative
  - 20% company/role language
  - 10% light technical warmup
- If next round is technical coding:
  - 60% coding reps
  - 25% behavioral follow-ups
  - 15% light design communication
- If next round is system design:
  - 65% design drills
  - 25% behavioral leadership stories
  - 10% coding maintenance
- If next round is panel/final:
  - 50% behavioral depth
  - 35% role-specific technical walkthroughs
  - 15% question strategy and closing

Rule: only prepare one round ahead plus light maintenance for others.

## 48-hour sprint before each round

- T-48 to T-24:
  - run 2 focused mocks for the exact round type
  - tighten weak answers and transitions
- T-24 to T-0:
  - light review only (hooks, metrics, structure)
  - no heavy new topics

## 30-45 minute execution format (default)

- 60 sec: intro + fit thesis
- 4-6 answers: 45-60 sec each, metric included
- 1 deeper follow-up story ready: 90 sec max
- 2-3 sharp questions for close (team, ownership scope, success criteria)

## Rolling plan (no fixed interview date)

Use this as a repeating weekly loop until interviews are scheduled.

Time model:
- Standard day: 2-3 hours (default)
- Deep-work day: up to 5 hours (only for complex topics)
- Cadence: 5 study days + 1 light review day + 1 rest day

## Deep-work day template (up to 5 hours)

- 90-120 min: low-cognitive research block (terms, domain topics, architecture patterns)
- 90 min: applied architecture walkthrough (timed + spoken)
- 60 min: behavioral refinement + follow-up defenses

## Efficiency rules

- Only do deep-work days when a topic blocks multiple rounds (high ROI)
- End each session with one concrete artifact (answer draft, design outline, or solved problem)
- Track misses, not just hours; next session starts with top miss
- Prefer timed reps over passive reading after initial understanding

- Focus patterns first, then speed.

## System design ramp (fast comfort ladder)

Use this progression so you build comfort quickly, not randomly.

- Level 1 (first 3 reps): structure first
  - focus on: requirements, APIs, data model, bottlenecks
  - output: one-page design skeleton each rep
- Level 2 (next 4 reps): reliability + operations
  - focus on: retries, idempotency, backpressure, observability
  - output: failure-mode list + mitigation plan
- Level 3 (next 4 reps): tradeoffs + evolution
  - focus on: MVP vs v2, vendor vs build, DynamoDB vs relational
  - output: 2-minute tradeoff summary you can say out loud
- Level 4 (ongoing): timed simulation
  - 35-45 min full design drills with spoken narration
  - end with interviewer-style recap and "what I'd improve next"

Target milestone: after ~11 reps, you should feel process-level comfort even if topic changes.
